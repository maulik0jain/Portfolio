About Me

I'm a Mechanical Engineering student at Delhi Technological University, currently Airframes Technician at UAS-DTU, DTU's official aerial systems team. I work at the intersection of aerodynamics, structural engineering, and autonomous systems — designing and building the hardware that lets our machines fly and survive in the real world.

At UAS-DTU, I built a fixed-wing RC aircraft from scratch: full aerodynamic analysis in XFLR5, structural validation through ANSYS FEA, and hands-on flight testing where I diagnosed and resolved pitch instability by iterating on the centre-of-gravity configuration. I also design rover chassis, sensor-mounting plates, and a 3-axis robotic arm in SolidWorks, while contributing to our FAST-LIO2 and VINS-Fusion stack running on Pixhawk Cube Orange with RTK-GPS. Our team is currently preparing for the DARPA Triage Challenge — autonomous casualty search and triage in GPS-degraded, communication-constrained environments.

Beyond mechanical design, I'm building a deep learning pipeline for non-contact vital sign detection at a 2-metre standoff — combining my hardware intuition with applied ML to solve real diagnostic problems. I've also completed Prof. A.K. Ghosh's aircraft stability and control course at IIT Kanpur, which gave me a rigorous foundation in flight dynamics that directly informs my design work.

My engineering toolkit spans SolidWorks, ANSYS, XFLR5, Python, ROS, PyTorch, and Mission Planner/PX4. I'm drawn to problems that sit on the boundary between the physical and the intelligent — where structural integrity, aerodynamic behaviour, and autonomous decision-making have to work together under real-world constraints.

Outside technical work, I enjoy analysing aircraft configurations, following the latest in drone autonomy research, and finding ways to make things lighter, stronger, and faster.
